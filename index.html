About Stain Glass

Stained glass is an art form that combines color, light, and craftsmanship to create decorative pieces made from cut glass assembled into a design. Traditionally used in cathedrals and historical buildings, stained glass has evolved into a modern art medium found in homes, galleries, and community exhibits.

Further insight into Stained Glass

  1. The Process of Making Stained Glass
    • Artists begin with a full-size pattern or drawing called a cartoon.
    • Individual pieces of colored glass are chosen based on hue, texture, and opacity.
    • Each piece is carefully cut and ground to fit the design.
  2. Techniques Used in Stain Glass
    • Tiffany (copper foil) method, where each piece of glass is wrapped in thin copper tape before being soldered together.
    • Lead came method, which uses H-shaped channels of lead to connect the glass pieces.
    • Kiln-fusing, where glass is melted into new shapes or textures for modern-style artwork.
  3. Why Stained Glass Is Unique
    • It interacts with natural and artificial light, changing appearance throughout the day.
    • No two pieces of glass are ever exactly the same, making each artwork one-of-a-kind.
    • It blends art, craftsmanship, and structural precision, requiring both creativity and technical skill.
